June 20 update

The June 20 update incorporates polling published through June 17, 88 days before the election. Since the SCB release, new Sifo, Ipsos, and Novus polls continue to show C+S+V+MP ahead, but with a slightly narrower margin. The latest Novus poll has C+S+V+MP at 54.0%, compared to 44.4% for M+L+KD+SD. The Liberal Party (L) remains below the threshold at 2.2%, while the Christian Democrats (KD) are above it at 5.6%. These results keep the model leaning clearly toward the opposition, but pull it back slightly from the June 4 update: C+S+V+MP's election-day forecast falls to 53.9%, the Tidö bloc rises to 44.2%, and the probability of a C+S+V+MP majority among parties above 4% decreases from 93.2% to 92.4%.

Notably, S+V+MP alone now achieves a majority in 54.0% of simulations, down from 60.2%. This is mainly because the model estimate for S falls by about 1.1 percentage points, while V and MP rise somewhat. The threshold calculations still matter: L remains projected to stay below 4% in most simulations, while KD is now more often above the threshold. In this analysis, votes for parties below the threshold are excluded prior to the majority assessment.

Methodology

How to read this forecast

How the updating works

The model starts with a reasonable prior view of party support, then updates that view when polls arrive.

Model

The model is based on Stoetzer et al. (2019). Early in the campaign, it leans more on earlier elections; closer to Election Day, new polls can move the forecast more.

Data

Polling inputs come from SwedishPolls. Official election results used for historical comparison come from Statistics Sweden.

Scope

The dashboard estimates national vote shares. For coalition questions, it applies a simple 4% threshold rule before checking who would have a majority among the modeled parties.
